What to mention

Useful request notes before comparing provider responses.

Use these prompts to describe the project in a practical way. You do not need perfect measurements or final design choices — clear context is enough.

01

Project category

Which part of the kitchen are you focused on?

Mention whether this is a full kitchen remodel, cabinet update, countertop request, backsplash work, lighting change, island planning, storage improvement, or a mix of several areas.

Good to include

Primary category Secondary concerns Unsure details
02

Kitchen size

Give a simple sense of room scale.

Approximate kitchen size, cabinet count, counter length, island size, or layout type can help frame the request. A rough description is fine if exact measurements are not ready.

Good to include

Room shape Cabinet count Counter length
03

Current issue

Explain what feels outdated, damaged, or difficult to use.

Describe the reason for the request: worn surfaces, poor lighting, awkward storage, damaged tile, limited prep space, dated cabinets, or a layout that no longer fits daily use.

Good to include

Main problem Daily frustration Visible damage
04

Style direction

Use a few words to describe the look you prefer.

Warm modern, classic cream, Scandinavian, olive cabinetry, light wood, brass details, stone surfaces, matte black hardware, or natural textures can all help providers understand direction.

Good to include

Color mood Material tone Styles to avoid
05

Preferred materials

List the finishes that matter most.

Mention countertop ideas, cabinet finish, tile shape, grout tone, fixture style, hardware color, sink type, lighting preference, or backsplash coverage.

Good to include

Counter surface Cabinet finish Fixture tone
06

Timeline

Say how soon you are hoping to move forward.

A flexible planning request is different from a near-term update. Mention whether you are comparing options, planning for later, or trying to understand availability quickly.

Good to include

Flexible timing Near-term need Important dates
07

Budget comfort range

Share budget context only if you want to.

You do not have to provide a number. If you do, treat it as context only. Final pricing, written scope, warranty terms, licensing, insurance, and schedules should be reviewed directly.

Good to include

Optional range Quote questions Written terms
