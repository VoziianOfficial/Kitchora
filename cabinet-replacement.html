Ask Through Request

Service detail

Cabinet replacement requests can focus the conversation around storage, finish, style, and practical fit.

Cabinet replacement is often one of the most visible parts of a kitchen update. Homeowners may be comparing new cabinet boxes, door fronts, drawer upgrades, hardware, pantry storage, shelving, soft-close features, or a completely different visual direction. Kitchora helps organize those details before provider responses are reviewed.

This category is useful when cabinetry is the primary concern, even if other kitchen details may be discussed later. Participating providers may ask about measurements, existing cabinet condition, material preferences, installation requirements, finish selections, and timeline. Those provider-specific details should be reviewed directly.

Kitchora is not a cabinet installer, cabinet retailer, design studio, or manufacturer. Final scope, pricing, product selections, warranties, licensing, insurance, and service terms are provided by participating providers.

What to include

Details that can help frame a cabinet replacement request.

You do not need final cabinet drawings to submit a request. Basic notes can help participating providers understand whether the discussion is about appearance, storage, layout, or a deeper replacement scope.

Approximate number of cabinets, drawers, or cabinet walls
Whether the existing layout should stay similar or change
Preferred cabinet style, color, finish, and hardware tone
Storage frustrations, pantry goals, drawer needs, and access concerns
Whether counters, backsplash, lighting, or flooring may also be affected
Questions about measurements, written scope, warranties, insurance, and cleanup
